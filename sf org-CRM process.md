Interviewer: Can you explain the CRM process with a simple example?

Answer:

Let's take a Hyundai showroom as an example.

1. Lead – A customer, Ravi, visits the showroom or fills out an online form showing interest in buying a Hyundai Creta. We create a Lead.

2. Lead Conversion – After confirming that Ravi is genuinely interested, we convert the Lead into:

   * Account – Ravi (or his company)
   * Contact – Ravi's personal details
   * Opportunity – The potential car sale

3. Opportunity – The sales team follows the sales process:
   Qualification → Needs Analysis → Proposal/Quote → Negotiation → Closed Won

4. Product – Add the Hyundai Creta to the Opportunity.

5. Quote – Send a quotation for ₹15 lakhs to the customer.

6. Order & Invoice – Once Ravi accepts the quote, create the Order and generate the Invoice.

7. Case – After purchasing the car, if Ravi reports a service issue, create a Case for customer support.

### Easy CRM Flow

Lead → Account + Contact + Opportunity → Product → Quote → Order → Invoice → Case

One-line interview answer:

> "CRM is the process of managing the customer journey from the first inquiry to the sale and after-sales support. In Salesforce, we typically follow: Lead → Account → Contact → Opportunity → Product → Quote → Order → Invoice → Case."
